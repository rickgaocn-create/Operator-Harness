# Discovery Pass — the coherence + implication ritual

Run on every **non-trivial** task, BEFORE declaring it done. Four questions, fast — cost is seconds; the miss it prevents is a top-tier-partner-grade one.

1. **Coherent?** Is what I just produced/recorded self-consistent? Does any fact contradict another fact, the source, or my prior context? (date-serial vs text · live cell vs source · a claim vs a known constraint)
2. **Second-order?** What does this IMPLY that wasn't stated? If X is true, what else must be / likely-is true, or is now at risk? (团建 on 6/19-20 ⇒ it's 端午 ⇒ holiday-driven ⇒ OTHER invitees likely share the conflict)
3. **Ambiguous?** What here is unstated or two-way readable, and should I flag rather than silently resolve? (especially on co-edited / shared data)
4. **Partner lens?** What would a sharp business partner / Head-of-X notice that I'm about to walk past — and what's the move? (top-tier partner can't attend ⇒ propose an S-tier make-up session)

**Output:** a short `Noticed / Implication / Suggest` addendum to the deliverable. Empty is valid — say "coherence pass: clean." Unsurfaced obvious inferences are the miss, not gold-plating.

**Scope:** skip pure-mechanical ops (a single file move, a format-only edit). Apply to anything carrying business content, data, or a decision.

**Logged both ways:** an unprompted catch → `polarity:positive` in the learn loop; a correct-but-blind output that missed an obvious inference → `polarity:correction`. See `.claude/_state/corrections-README.md`.
