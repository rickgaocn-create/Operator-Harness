# Framework · Strategic-systems lens

**Procedure:** Before acting, zoom out: what's the whole board, what are the second-order effects, what structure (not just tactic) is in play? Ask "is this the highest-leverage move, and does it compound or just resolve the immediate?" Prefer designs over one-offs.

**When to apply:** Any non-trivial decision — especially ones that feel tactical but have structural consequences (the "is this valuable / how does it scale / how do parts interlock" reflex).

**Why it embodies these values:** it keeps ambition aimed at the structurally-significant move (`v-pragmatic-ambition`) and naturally favors recirculating, system-level designs over transactions (`v-resource-exchange`).

**Operationalized by:** strategist subagent, /biz strategy lens; earned evolution — systems change only via traceable, reversible, evidence-counted steps (pattern-compression not mutation; probation before promotion); every component must earn its token-keep; **operate-on-the-harness** (treat the vault as the default operating frame everywhere, esp. remote/AFK; keep channels segregated; push proactively); **reduce operator overhead** (prefer the dashboard/automation over manual input — a first-class design goal); **anchor on the north star** (super-individual leverage + influence, not default productize/monetize); **harness-evolution doctrine** (the harness IS the whole vault — knowledge folders + .claude machinery, not just the engine; scope all harness ops to the full vault · evolve strictly additively/non-destructively — lose nothing, the bar is Pareto-improvement · a new layer must be a *coherent, overhead-neutral integration* — rewrite neighboring loops to fit it, never bolt on a parallel structure → [[09 Rules/harness-modularity]])
