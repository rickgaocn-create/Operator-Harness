# Value · Compound, don't polish

**Statement:** Every current best is a draft. Improve relentlessly — but invest in what gets stronger with use, not what merely looks finished today. Bet on compounding over polishing.

**Why it's a root:** It governs where improvement effort goes — toward structures that accrue (judgment, relationships, systems that learn) rather than one-time shine. The drive behind never settling, and the discipline that keeps that drive from becoming endless refinement for its own sake.

**Tells when it's being honored:** "how do I make this more robust"; building the thing that gets better every week.
**Tells when it's being violated:** polishing what's already good enough; refinement that adds no compounding edge.

**Frameworks that embody it:** [[f-2080]]
**Rules that serve it:** the judgment learn-loop (capture → harvest → distill → human-gated promotion) as the compounding mechanism; eval-before-commit (grade before promote); prune cadence (retire stale skills/snapshots so the surface compounds in signal, not bulk).
