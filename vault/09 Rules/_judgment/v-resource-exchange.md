# Value · Exchange over spend

**Statement:** Don't spend what you can exchange. Architect structures where value recirculates and both sides come out ahead — then find the loop everyone else missed.

**Why it's a root:** It's the operating model under the BD and M&A work — favoring designs where value compounds for both parties over designs that pay once. Shapes what a "good" deal, partnership, or resource trade even looks like before any tactic is chosen.

**Tells when it's being honored:** a partnership built as a recirculating loop, not a transaction; turning an owned asset into leverage instead of cash.
**Tells when it's being violated:** evaluating a deal only on its one-time payoff; spending where a trade was available; a structure only one side wins.
